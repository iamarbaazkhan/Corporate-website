How IP creates a valuable Brand?

1. What are different IP rights?

- The five IP rights are: • Trademark – Protects distinctive signs of a business.
• Copyright – Protects cultural, artistic and literary works.
• Patent – Protects innovative products and processes.
• Designs – Protects creative designs including textile designs.
• Geographical Indications - Protects Traditional Products specific for a geographical region.


2. What is Trademark?
- Trademark is a word, sign or symbol capable of distinguishing products of one business enterprise to that of another business enterprise.

3. What are the advantages of getting a Trademark?
- Avoid Duplication stop infringement.
- Huge brand image and publicity.
- Own your business name / logo legally.
- Franchise / Lease your name and earn royalty.
- Grow with your brand and assets.

4. How should be your brand name?
- Attributes of a good brand –
✔ Create a unique and distinctive name – Apple for computers and Diesel for Jeans are well thought unique names
✔Avoid descriptive names like Freeze Air for air conditioners as it is very common
✔Avoid personal names and surnames.
✔Avoid similarity to an existing name – anyways that illegal
✔Avoid name of God and regions of world
✔Try having the name short and unique as FedEx for Federal Express is quiet appreciated.

5. Is your brand name associated with an artistic design or a Logo?
- Strong impact of your Goodwill and Reputation is carried ahead by a unique design of your name or logo if you have one go ahead!
